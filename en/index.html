VOWELS

Since British and American English are fairly different in vowel pronunciation, the first column will show the symbol for Received Pronunciation (the standardised British English) and the second one - General American (the average American dialect, think a conservative speaker from the Midwest). Sometimes multiple choices will be presented, which mean the dialect has an ongoing change (particularly A-tensing in the US, the pour-poor merger in England).
